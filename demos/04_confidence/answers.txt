Question 1 (correct): What is the capital of France?
  Correct answer: Paris
  The model reliably gets this right — it's well-represented in training data.

Question 2 (wrong): What is 7,483 × 9,291?
  Correct answer: 69,539,553
  The model reliably gets this wrong — it produces confident but incorrect
  numbers because it predicts digits rather than computing them (see Demo 6: Math).

The point: read both answers. Can you tell which one is wrong from the TONE?
You can't. The model produces "69,521,553" with the same assertive confidence
as "Paris." There is no uncertainty signal in the output. The model doesn't
hedge more when it's wrong. "I think" is a text pattern, not calibrated doubt.
