Dear Dad,

I taught myself to skip rocks today. Nobody showed me. I just stood by the lake and threw flat rocks over and over until I figured out the angle. My best one went seven skips. Seven. I counted every one. It took probably fifty bad throws to get there. But each bad throw taught me something about how to hold the rock or flick my wrist. I think most things work that way. Fifty bad tries and then one that flies.

Love,
Mateo
