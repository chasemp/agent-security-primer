Dear Dad,

I'm coming home tomorrow and I'm excited to see you but also I don't want to leave. I didn't expect that. I thought I'd be counting the days. Instead I'm trying to slow them down. I learned to swim and build fires and read maps and tie knots. But mostly I learned that I can do hard things. Not that they stop being hard. They don't. I just stop letting the hardness be a reason not to try. I'll see you at pickup. Save me a window seat for the drive home.

Love,
Marcus
